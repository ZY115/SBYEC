## What's New (User Facing)
 * A set of step-by-step manuals and tutorial materials were created to help the client independently manage and update key sections of the SBYEC website.
 * New documentation explains how to add and manage horses on the Meet the Herd page, including how to upload a featured image and add descriptive information for each horse.
 * A tutorial video was created demonstrating how to add new horses to the herd page, ensuring that the client can update the page easily once new horse photos and information become available.
 * A tutorial video was also created explaining how to adjust team member photo sizes and maintain consistent formatting on the Meet Our Team page.
 * A video guide was added showing how to add sponsor logos and maintain proper spacing on the Sponsors & Partners page.
 * Written instructions were created explaining how to update the event image and date on the Books at the Buckle page, allowing the client to easily update future program announcements.
 * Updated the chatbot UI to make the interaction area cleaner and easier for users to read

## Work Summary (Developer Facing)
During this sprint, our team focused on translating the client’s detailed feedback into actionable documentation and support materials that improve the maintainability of the SBYEC website. After reviewing the client’s questions and requested updates, we analyzed how different WordPress components were structured, including the team member system, the herd page layout, and the sponsor logo sections. This required exploring how content types, categories, and media assets interact with the page layouts used by the site. A key challenge was identifying the correct workflow that the client could realistically follow without developer assistance. To address this, we experimented with several update methods in the WordPress dashboard and then documented the most stable and user-friendly process. We then converted these processes into clear manuals and tutorial videos so the client can independently perform tasks such as updating event content, managing team member ordering, and adding new animals or sponsors. Through this work, our team gained a deeper understanding of maintaining client-facing WordPress systems and learned how to design documentation that bridges the gap between technical implementation and non-technical users.

## Retrospective Summary
Here's what went well:
  * Communication with the client went smoothly this sprint. The client provided clear feedback and detailed questions, which helped us quickly identify the areas that needed improvement or additional guidance.
  * Our team successfully addressed the client’s requests by creating manuals and tutorial videos that explain how to perform common website updates. This ensures the client can manage the site more independently.
  * Team collaboration was effective, allowing us to divide tasks such as testing WordPress update workflows, preparing documentation, and recording tutorial materials without blocking each other.
 
Here's what we'd like to improve:
   * We would like to further refine the clarity and organization of our documentation so that it is even easier for non-technical users to follow.
   * We plan to improve our internal process for validating instructions before delivering them to the client to ensure that each step is clear and reproducible.
   
  
Here are changes we plan to implement in the next sprint:
   * We plan to check in with the client to see if there are additional website management tasks that would benefit from new tutorials or manuals.
   * We will continue improving the usability of the documentation so that the client, as a non-technical user, can comfortably manage updates to the website.
   * Based on the client’s feedback, we will determine whether additional adjustments or small feature improvements are needed.
